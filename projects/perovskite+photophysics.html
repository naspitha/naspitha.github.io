Perovskite Photophysics

This is a developing page that summarizes my work in the groups of John Wright and Song Jin at UW-Madison. More content coming soon! For a detailed view of the various projects, you can check out the following publications:

  • Discerning between Exciton and Free-Carrier Behaviors in Ruddlesden–Popper Perovskite Quantum Wells through Kinetic Modeling of Photoluminescence Dynamics, where we argue that time-resolved photoluminescence data is best analyzed with sets of rate equations, and that free carriers should also be taken into account even in a highly confined system like RP perovskites!
  • Deterministic fabrication of arbitrary vertical heterostructures of two-dimensional Ruddlesden–Popper halide perovskites, where my group shows off a neat way to synthesize perovskite heterostructures of any desired composition
  • Distinct Carrier Transport Properties Across Horizontally vs Vertically Oriented Heterostructures of 2D/3D Perovskites, where we characterize the nature of carrier transfer between the 2D and 3D components of a perovskite heterostructure and its dependence on orientation and thickness.
