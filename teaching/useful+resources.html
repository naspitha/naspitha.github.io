This is a list of various resources that I consider to have been useful in my teaching and learning journey. This list is mostly for my own use, as I will often go back to these resources, but I'm sure someone else will find it useful!

  • Inventory of inclusive teaching practices by University of Michigan
  • CIRTL MOOC Website (stemteachingcourse.org); contains short videos that nicely summarize a variety of evidence-based teaching approaches.
  • For UW-Madison faculty and students: Go to https://dataviz.wisc.edu/ (log-in required) and search for "Subject/Course Summary" to see how your department/course is doing in terms of D/F/Drop rates of specific demographics.
  • A checklist for universal design of instruction from University of Washington
  • Google Doc summarizing take-aways from a CIRTL seminar on Leading Online Discussion.
