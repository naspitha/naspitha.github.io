About Me

I am a scientist who has spent most of her graduate school time researching spectroscopy of solar-energy materials, but also finds herself increasingly passionate about chemistry education research. When not in the lab, I also identify as a dedicated karateka, musician, and (when time allows) artist! This website was initially created to host my teaching portfolio as part of the Delta program at UW-Madison, but it is also a starting point linking to my research and other professional projects. As of 2022, I have accepted a fellowship by the Alexander von Humboldt Foundation to pursue a postdoc in chemistry education research at the Tiemann Lab at the Humboldt University of Berlin.

Projects

Perovskite Photophysics

Lead halide perovskites are an emerging technology in the design of solar cells and optoelectronics. They are efficient and inexpensive to synthesize, but can have shortcomings in their stability. As attempts are constantly made to synthesize new perovskites that balance high performance with stability, my research focuses on characterizing their charge carrier behavior using spectroscopy.

Beer's Law Simulation

This simulation was developed to give undergraduate analytical chemistry students a more intuitive explanation as to why Beer's law (i.e. absorbance being directly proportional to concentration) holds. We are currently investigating the effects of an activity based on this simulation on students' reasoning about the phenomenon of absorption.

KinetiKit

KinetiKit is a Python-based software package that I developed during my graduate work. It can be used to analyze temporal data (in my case, time-resolved photoluminescence of semiconductors) using models of any desired complexity that represent the physical behavior of particles in a system.
